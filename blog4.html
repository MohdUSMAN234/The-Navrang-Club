Importance of Cutural Activities in Technical Studies

Previously, collegelife used to be lectures and attendance. Now, student's engagement and their preparation towards the real world depends on the cultural fest and events. It encourages them to participate and showcase their talent. The advanced cultural events, technical fests, performance and competitions with friends make an engineering student confident and job-ready. College fests play an important role in shaping the career of a student. Companies prefer hiring candidates of the best Engineering Colleges who are knowledgeable, expert in their field. have good communication skills and are interactive with clients. If a candidate has been an active participant in college fest, cultural activities, sports tournaments or anything outside the classroom then it shows their latent ad ability. If students who wish to pursue higher studies, these co-curricular activities make them different from others.

Involvement in college fest along with studies teaches students to manage time efficienty, prioritize work, follow schedule, and honor deadlines. Fest and events at Engineering College provide many opportunities to interact with your peers and form new connections. While organizing any fest, students stumble upon new people and build their strong networks. Most of these activities are GD by which students get to know about people from different cutural backgrounds and interests. These healthy interactions not only enhance the interpersonal communication skills but also prepares them for the real world. Technical fest provides a platform for young brains to showcase their innovative ideas and compete with their peers.

Those who can multi-task, take initiative, and are enterprising have better chances of getting hired. students grow in multiple directions if they actively participate in college fest. Students who volunteer and organize college fest display their self-started attitude, which is highly appreciated by recruiters, specially while hiring for administrative positions.
